1. Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone

The story begins when a baby, Harry Potter, survives a mysterious attack by the dark wizard Voldemort. Harry’s parents, James and Lily Potter, are killed that night, but Voldemort’s killing curse somehow rebounds and destroys his body, leaving only a lightning-shaped scar on Harry’s forehead. The infant Harry is taken by the half-giant Rubeus Hagrid to live with his only relatives, the Dursley family, who are ordinary non-magical people (Muggles). Harry grows up in misery under the care of Vernon Dursley, Petunia Dursley, and their spoiled son Dudley Dursley. They hide the truth about his parents and treat him like a servant.

When Harry turns eleven, mysterious letters begin arriving addressed to him, but the Dursleys desperately try to prevent him from reading them. Eventually Hagrid personally delivers Harry’s letter inviting him to study at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. Harry learns he is famous in the wizarding world as “The Boy Who Lived.” Hagrid takes him to buy magical supplies at Diagon Alley, where Harry first learns about the wizarding society and sees the bank vault containing a mysterious package that Hagrid retrieves.

At Hogwarts, Harry meets his closest friends: Ron Weasley, a kind but poor wizard boy, and Hermione Granger, a brilliant Muggle-born witch. Harry is sorted into Gryffindor house by the magical Sorting Hat. Throughout the year Harry becomes skilled at Quidditch, learns magic, and begins to suspect that a powerful magical object called the Philosopher's Stone—which grants immortality—is hidden in the school.

After many adventures and clues, Harry discovers that the timid professor Quirinus Quirrell is secretly serving Voldemort, whose weakened spirit is attached to the back of Quirrell’s head. Quirrell tries to steal the Stone to restore Voldemort to power, but Harry stops him. Harry’s touch burns Quirrell because of the magical protection his mother left when she sacrificed herself for him. The Stone is destroyed to prevent misuse, and Harry finishes his first year realizing the danger Voldemort still poses.

2. Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets

Harry returns to Hogwarts for his second year but soon discovers something dark is happening. Students begin being mysteriously petrified, and frightening messages appear on the walls saying the legendary Chamber of Secrets has been opened. According to Hogwarts history, one of the founders, Salazar Slytherin, built a hidden chamber within the school containing a monster meant to purge Hogwarts of students with non-magical heritage.

Harry begins hearing strange voices in the walls that no one else can hear. Eventually he realizes he has the rare ability to speak Parseltongue, the language of snakes, which frightens others because it is associated with dark wizards. Meanwhile, his friend Hermione discovers that the creature attacking students is a Basilisk—a gigantic serpent that kills with its gaze.

The attacks are secretly being controlled by a magical diary that possesses students. The diary belongs to Tom Riddle, a memory of Voldemort as a teenage student preserved in the book. The diary manipulates Ginny Weasley to reopen the Chamber. Harry enters the hidden chamber beneath the school, confronts the Basilisk, and defeats it using the sword of Godric Gryffindor. He destroys the diary with a Basilisk fang, unknowingly destroying the first Horcrux—an object containing a fragment of Voldemort’s soul.

3. Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban

During Harry’s third year, the wizarding world is terrified by the escape of the dangerous prisoner Sirius Black from the wizard prison Azkaban. Black was believed to have betrayed Harry’s parents to Voldemort and murdered several people. The terrifying prison guards known as Dementor are stationed around Hogwarts to protect the school. Their presence affects Harry more strongly than anyone else because they force him to relive the memory of his parents’ deaths.

Harry gradually learns that Sirius Black was actually his father’s best friend. The real traitor is revealed to be Peter Pettigrew, another friend who betrayed the Potters and framed Sirius. Pettigrew has been hiding for years disguised as Ron’s pet rat. Harry and Hermione use a magical device called a Time-Turner to travel back in time and rescue Sirius from execution, though Pettigrew escapes to rejoin Voldemort.

4. Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire

In Harry’s fourth year, Hogwarts hosts the dangerous Triwizard Tournament, a magical competition between three wizarding schools. The magical Goblet of Fire selects champions to compete, but unexpectedly it chooses Harry despite him being too young.

The tournament consists of deadly challenges including dragons, underwater rescues, and navigating a magical maze. Harry forms friendships with the other champions, especially Cedric Diggory. However, the final task turns into a trap. The tournament trophy is secretly transformed into a portkey that transports Harry and Cedric to a graveyard.

There, Voldemort’s followers kill Cedric and perform a dark ritual that restores Voldemort to full physical form. Harry narrowly escapes with Cedric’s body. This marks the true beginning of the Second Wizarding War.

5. Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix

After Voldemort’s return, the Ministry of Magic refuses to believe Harry and Albus Dumbledore, fearing public panic. To control Hogwarts, the Ministry appoints the cruel professor Dolores Umbridge, who gradually takes control of the school and punishes students harshly.

Harry secretly forms a group called Dumbledore’s Army to teach students defensive magic. Meanwhile Harry experiences strange visions connected to Voldemort’s mind. Believing Voldemort is torturing Sirius Black, Harry and his friends rush to the Department of Mysteries in the Ministry of Magic. A battle breaks out between Voldemort’s followers and members of the Order of the Phoenix. During the fight Sirius is killed by Bellatrix Lestrange.

Dumbledore later reveals an ancient prophecy stating that neither Harry nor Voldemort can live while the other survives.

6. Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince

Harry’s sixth year focuses on uncovering Voldemort’s past. Dumbledore shows Harry memories revealing how Tom Riddle gradually became Voldemort and learned to split his soul into multiple pieces using dark magic. These soul fragments are hidden in objects called Horcruxes.

Harry discovers a mysterious potions textbook owned by someone calling themselves the “Half-Blood Prince,” which contains advanced magical knowledge. Meanwhile Draco Malfoy is secretly working for Voldemort and trying to assassinate Dumbledore.

At the end of the book, Dumbledore and Harry retrieve a Horcrux from a hidden cave. When they return to Hogwarts, Dumbledore is disarmed by Draco but ultimately killed by Severus Snape, who then reveals himself as the Half-Blood Prince and flees.

7. Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows

In the final book, Harry, Ron, and Hermione leave Hogwarts to hunt down and destroy Voldemort’s Horcruxes. The wizarding world falls under Voldemort’s control, and the trio spends months traveling in hiding while searching for the remaining soul fragments.

They also learn about three legendary magical objects called the Deathly Hallows: the Elder Wand, the Resurrection Stone, and the Invisibility Cloak.

Eventually they return to Hogwarts where the final battle occurs between Voldemort’s forces and the defenders of the school. Harry learns the shocking truth that a piece of Voldemort’s soul lives inside him, making him an unintended Horcrux. Harry willingly sacrifices himself to destroy that soul fragment, but he survives because Voldemort unknowingly used Harry’s blood in his resurrection.

In the final duel, Voldemort’s killing curse rebounds because the Elder Wand truly belongs to Harry, destroying Voldemort forever.
